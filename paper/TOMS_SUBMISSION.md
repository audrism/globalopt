## Cover letter (paste into the cover-letter box)

Dear Editors,

We submit "Wrap or Rewrite? Reviving the 1989 GlobalMinimum Bayesian
Global-Optimization Library for R and Python" for consideration as a
research contribution in ACM TOMS.

The paper modernizes Jonas Mockus's 1989 GlobalMinimum library along
both standard routes -- wrapping the original compiled Fortran behind R
and Python packages, and translating the core methods to Rust -- and
instruments the difference. Its contributions are (1) the packages
themselves, in which every optimizer runs the original algorithms with
modern safety, seeding, and tracing; (2) a translation-fidelity study
that resolves an apparent 12x performance anomaly to two dropped
pruning rules, restores bitwise trajectory agreement, and documents
correctness defects (transposed test-function matrices, an upstream
uninitialized read, a harness constant-shadowing bug) that only
executable-to-executable comparison caught -- a discipline we argue is
directly relevant to today's LLM-assisted translation practice; and
(3) a 78,000-run benchmark of the 1989 methods against modern global
optimizers in both ecosystems, externally validated on BBOB/COCO, with
a compound method (EXKOR/A2) built from the library's own components
that trades aggregate throughput for markedly fewer total failures.

We request RCR review: every number and figure in the paper is
regenerated by scripts in the public repository, the benchmark
instances are bit-identical across the R and Python harnesses, and the
package test suites pin trace-level reference values against the 1989
binary.

The submitting author discloses that the original library's author is
his father; this is also stated in the acknowledgments.

The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. A preprint will
be posted to arXiv (math.OC).

Sincerely,
Audris Mockus
